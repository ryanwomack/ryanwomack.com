Posted: 2025-10-21   |   Tags: Academic Freedom, blog, Universities

Academic freedom is in the news. Why is it called academic freedom? Does it derive from the expression, “it’s purely academic”? As in definition number 5 here: You can say that a discussion or situation is academic if you think it is not important because it has no real effect or cannot happen. Yet looking around, one sees many who express a desire to define academic freedom differently, or at least to appear to do so, in order for the semblance of the concept to take on a shimmering soaring effect, regardless of the source of the sheen.
