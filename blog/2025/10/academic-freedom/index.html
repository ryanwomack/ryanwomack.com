'Academic' Freedom

Posted: 2025-10-21   |   Tags: Academic Freedom, blog, Universities

Academic freedom is in the news. Why is it called academic freedom? Does it derive from the expression, “it’s purely academic”? As in definition number 5 here:

You can say that a discussion or situation is academic if you think it is not important because it has no real effect or cannot happen.

Yet looking around, one sees many who express a desire to define academic freedom differently, or at least to appear to do so, in order for the semblance of the concept to take on a shimmering soaring effect, regardless of the source of the sheen.

This academic freedom, what does it consist of? Does one fence in one’s thought within narrow disciplinary or institutional bounds and then claim freedom within those confines? Or can an opinion be expressed as long as one introduces it with a laundry list of caveats and disclaimers*, and voices it sufficiently softly and obscurely that its impact is appropriately muffled?

Is academic freedom to be exercised, like a muscle, or preserved, like a pickle?

*I have been strongly advised and admonished to state here that my opinion is solely my own and not that of my employer.
